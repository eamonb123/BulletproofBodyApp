### CEREALS (Realistic Bowl = 1.5 cups cereal + 3/4 cup 2% milk)

**Milk math:** 3/4 cup 2% milk = ~90 cal

The label serving sizes are deceptively small. Here's what the label says vs. a realistic bowl:

#### 5. Frosted Flakes - 1 REALISTIC BOWL WITH MILK

- **Label serving:** 1.5 cups (41g) = 150 cal dry
- **Realistic pour:** ~1.5 cups (people pour at least this)
- **1.5 cups dry:** ~225 cal (150 x 1.5 based on weight scaling; or ~1.5 servings worth)
- **+ 3/4 cup 2% milk:** ~90 cal
- **TOTAL REALISTIC BOWL:** ~315 cal
- **Current in DB:** 280 cal
- **Verdict:** Update to 315.
- **Note:** Label recently changed to 1.5 cup serving = 150 cal. But cereal is very light; 1.5 cups of Frosted Flakes is only 41g. People easily pour 1.5-2 cups.
- **Source:** [SmartLabel Kellogg's](https://smartlabel.wkkellogg.com/Product/Index?gtin=00038000146367), [CalorieKing](https://www.calorieking.com/us/en/foods/f/calories-in-breakfast-cereals-ready-to-eat-frosted-flakes-cereal/hfAEwWz8RJOMzXt4GaUb1A)

### NEW ITEM

#### 18. ALMONDS (Blue Diamond or similar) - TWO HANDFULS / SNACK BAG

- **1 handful = ~1 oz (28g) = ~23 almonds = 164 cal**
- **2 handfuls = ~2 oz (56g) = ~46 almonds = 328 cal**
- **Larger/generous handfuls (40g each) = 2 x 232 = ~464 cal**
- **1 cup almonds = ~660 cal**
- **Blue Diamond 100-cal packs = 0.6 oz per pack = 100 cal**
- **Standard snack bag (1.5 oz) = ~246 cal**
- **User claim of 600+ for two handfuls:** This is HIGH but possible if someone has very large hands or scoops rather than grabs. Two standard handfuls = ~330 cal. Two generous scoops = ~460 cal. To hit 600 you'd need ~3.5 oz (~100g), which is closer to a full cup.
- **Recommendation:** Use 330 cal for "two handfuls" (realistic grab). If user wants "a bag," typical grab bag (1.5 oz) = 250 cal.
- **Source:** [Harvard Nutrition Source](https://nutritionsource.hsph.harvard.edu/food-features/almonds/), [FatSecret](https://foods.fatsecret.com/calories-nutrition/usda/almonds), [Food Network](https://www.foodnetwork.com/healthyeats/healthy-tips/2013/04/how-many-almonds-in-a-serving)
