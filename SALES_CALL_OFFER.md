# Sales Call Offer — What You Present on the Strategy Call

> Source of truth for how to present Gold and Silver.
> Last updated: 2026-03-09
> Referenced by: `/lead-magnet-ask-company`, AWARENESS_LADDER.md, GOLD_SILVER_AUDIT.md
> Rule: Joel must be bought in on the PROCESS and OUTCOME before you present a choice or talk price.

---

## The Call Flow

```
PART 1: THE PROCESS (universal — both tiers)
  1. Paint the picture     — photos, no changes, finding the hidden calories
  2. The build             — Chipotle aha, I map everything, it goes in your app
  3. The outcome           — 1 lb/week, groceries to your door, life adapts

PART 2: THE CHOICE (only after they're bought in on the process)
  4. Two options           — "I build it for you" vs "we build it together"
  5. Gold first            — concierge, weekly calls, Slack, box to your door
  6. Silver second         — same system, community-driven, we build it together
  7. The close             — recommend based on what you heard, price, payment plan
```

**Part 1 has NO mention of Gold, Silver, tiers, or price.** You're just describing what happens. Joel is buying the process and outcome. Part 2 is how he gets it.

---

## PART 1: THE PROCESS

### Step 1: Paint the Picture

> "So here's how this works. You eat exactly how you've been eating. Change nothing. For two weeks, you just take a photo before every meal. That's it. No changes. No diet. No willpower. Just photos.
>
> I look at all of it. Every snack, every DoorDash order, every work lunch. And I find the spots where you're eating 400, 500, 600 extra calories and you had no idea. Not because you're doing anything wrong. Just because nobody ever showed you."

**Name his objection before he says it:**

> "You're probably thinking: I already know what to eat, I just don't do it. That's actually the whole point. You DO know. You just don't know where the hidden calories are. A tablespoon of olive oil is 120 calories. Two tablespoons of mayo is 200. A single bagel is 300. Most guys are leaking 500 calories a day from stuff they don't even think about."

---

### Step 2: The Build

> "After two weeks of photos, I come back with your complete system. Every restaurant you go to, every snack in your drawer, every grocery run. I've mapped it all.
>
> Here's your Chipotle bowl at 1,100 calories. Here's the same bowl, same restaurant, same vibe, 470 calories. You just saved 600 calories and didn't give up Chipotle.
>
> I do that for everything. It goes in your app. You open the app, tap to log, and the calories handle themselves."

**The key line:**

> "I can't give you a plan on day one. That's why the first two weeks exist. I need to see how YOU eat first."

---

### Step 3: The Outcome

> "The math is just math. 500 calories a day from swaps you don't even feel. One pound of fat a week. Every week. Around the food you already love.
>
> And as your life changes, the system changes with you. New restaurant? It gets added. New craving? I find the swap. Business trip? The restaurants near your hotel are mapped before you land. The system evolves with you."

---

### CHECKPOINT: Is Joel Bought In on the Process?

Joel should be nodding. Asking questions like "so how does the app work?" or "what if I travel a lot?" Those are buying signals. He sees himself in the process.

If he's not engaged, go deeper. Use his specific situation: "You mentioned you eat at [restaurant] a lot. That's exactly the kind of thing I'd map out."

**Do NOT move to Part 2 until he's bought in.**

## PART 2: THE CHOICE

### Step 4: Introduce Two Options

> "So I've got two ways we can do this. Both for my busy professionals, CEOs, entrepreneurs. Time is money.
>
> One where I build it entirely for you. That's my most hands-on package. And one where we build it together.
>
> Want me to start with the first one?"

(They always say yes.)

---

### Step 5: Gold — "I Build It for You"

> "This is for my busy CEOs and entrepreneurs who don't have time to figure this out themselves. I handle everything.
>
> I take your photos, I analyze them, I build the entire system, and I present it to you. You tell me what you like, what you'd change, and I adjust it. You don't build anything. I build it. You just approve.
>
> Every week we hop on a call. 15 minutes. I look at the data, you tell me what came up, we adjust on the fly. Between calls, you can message me anytime on Slack. It integrates with your work environment. Question at 2pm about what to order at the client dinner? Answered before you sit down.
>
> If you go quiet, I check in. If something's off, I name it directly. No empty praise. I send you personalized video reviews when your form, app, or plan needs attention. Every Sunday I review your data and set the direction for the week.
>
> You also get the community. Three calls a week with other guys going through the same thing. Engineers, founders, execs. So you're not doing this alone."

**The box (the kicker, at the end of Gold):**

> "And to make this super simple for you, I ship everything to your door before we even start. Scale, food scale, fitness tracker. All compatible with my system. No extra charge. You don't have to figure out what to buy or what's compatible. It's all included in the package and it's ready to go."

**Why the box comes last:** Joel's already thinking "I want that." The box is the detail that says "this guy handles EVERYTHING, down to the equipment." It's the cherry on top that separates this from anything he's ever seen from a coach.

---

### Step 6: Silver — "We Build It Together"

> "The second option is the same system. Same tools. Same methodology. Same outcome: a pound a week around the food you love.
>
> The difference is we build it together instead of me building it for you. You've got the community behind you. Three group calls a week where I'm coaching live. Slack channel where I'm in there every single day. You post your food logs, your questions, your wins, and I coach you in there.
>
> You also get two personal check-ins a year, just us, where we audit everything and make sure you're on track.
>
> This is for guys who are self-starters. You don't need someone holding your hand. You need the system, the community, and access to me when you need it."

---

### Step 7: The Close

> "Both get you to the same place. Losing a pound of fat a week around the food you already love. One I build it for you, the other we build it together.
>
> Honestly? Based on everything you've told me, I'd recommend [Gold/Silver] because [specific reason from the call].
>
> Gold is $7,000 for 12 weeks. Silver is $500 a month for 12 months. Which one sounds like the better fit for how you operate?"

**If price hesitation on Gold:**

> "There's a payment plan: three installments of $2,800."

> "You've probably spent more than $7,000 over the years on supplements, gym memberships, trainers, and diets that didn't work. This is the last one. And unlike the others, I build it around YOUR life."

**If Silver hesitation on 12-month commitment:**

> "$500 a month. That's less than most guys spend on supplements they're not even sure work. And it's 12 months because it takes 12 months to make this a lifestyle, not a phase."

**Do not discount. Do not negotiate. Just normalize the number.**

## Placement Framework (from Coach Sparkle)

Listen for energy and need level during the call:

| Signal | Recommend |
|--------|-----------|
| "I'm done. I'm tired of this. I'm all in." | Gold |
| "I can't do this myself. I've failed too many times." | Gold |
| DISC D-type ("let's go" energy) | Gold |
| Steady, curious, reserved energy | Silver |
| "I know what to do, I just need accountability" | Silver |
| Past client who already knows the principles | Silver |

**The question:** "How much help do they need from me? If they need me to build it, Gold. If they need the system and community, Silver."

**Sparkle's script for Silver placement:**

> "Everything I've heard from you, I do have two options. One is more of a private, one-on-one-with-me experience. Everything I've heard, I believe you're going to benefit most in the group first. And then we can always move you up if that's what you want."

## What NOT to Say on the Call

- Don't say "concierge" or "ecosystem" (internal language)
- Don't say "Phase 1" or "Phase 2" (say "first two weeks" and "after that")
- Don't say "saboteur" (say "patterns" or "the thing that trips you up")
- Don't say "drift detection" (say "if you go quiet, I'll check in")
- Don't promise specific results ("most people see 1 lb/week" not "you'll lose 20 lbs")
- Don't promise 24/7 availability (same-day response, not instant)
- Don't mention the free tool on the call (they already used it)
- Don't list features. Describe what happens.

## The Funnel Context (How They Got Here)

By the time they're on the call, they've already:
1. Used the free tool and seen their own swap savings (proof)
2. Watched the VSL or read the concierge page (methodology + trust)
3. Booked a call (commitment signal)

They're bought in on the CONCEPT. The call is about:
- Can I trust this specific person (Eamon)?
- Does he understand MY situation?
- Which option fits me?
