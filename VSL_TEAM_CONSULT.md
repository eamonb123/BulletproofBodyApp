# VSL Team Consult (2026-03-08)

## Context

Entry point: user has already completed swap flow, seen calorie savings, seen projection, and submitted email.
This is a **warm, high-intent trust stage** (not cold traffic).

## Team Recommendations

### Maya (PM)
- Keep the page focused on one outcome: book the strategy call.
- Remove non-critical branching CTAs that dilute intent.
- Show exactly what happens next in 3 bullets.

### Kai (UX)
- Keep the visual hierarchy clean: one hero, one video, one CTA block.
- Reduce cognitive load: avoid dual-card decisions under the video.
- Ensure the CTA button is visible without extra scrolling on desktop.

### Lex (Copy)
- Use Joel language: "what you already eat", "your real life", "no meal prep".
- Avoid jargon-heavy phrasing on the first read.
- Speak to stage reality: they already saw proof; now they need implementation.

### Nick (Lead Magnet Strategist)
- Position this as a bridge to implementation, not a hard sell.
- Copy should reinforce: "If free tool gave this much clarity, what does full system do?"
- CTA should feel like the natural next step, not pressure.

### Remy (Dopamine / Momentum)
- Preserve momentum from the projection "win".
- Use action-forward language ("build", "map", "lock in") rather than passive language.
- Keep friction low: one button, one next action.

## What We Changed

1. Removed "Want the full script" secondary card.
2. Reframed hero for the post-swap stage.
3. Kept a single clean CTA: **Book Strategy Call**.
4. Added a "What Happens Next" section with concrete implementation steps.
