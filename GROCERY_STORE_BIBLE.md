# Grocery Store Bible

## Purpose

The Grocery Store Bible is the home-food layer of Bulletproof Body.

Fast Food Bible solves restaurant choices.
Snack Bible solves between-meal leaks.
Grocery Store Bible solves what the client buys and keeps at home.

## Core Problem

Clients say:

- "I do not know what to buy."
- "I know what to buy, but my grocery cart gets hijacked by convenience."
- "Delivery feels expensive, so I keep shopping inefficiently."

## Two Modules

### 1. Grocery Store Bible (What To Buy)

A curated list of high-satiety defaults across key categories:

- Protein anchors
- Carb anchors
- Fat anchors
- High-volume produce
- Convenience backups
- Sweet replacements
- Savory replacements

Each entry includes:

- Brand/product
- Serving size
- Calories
- Protein/carbs/fat
- Why it makes the cut
- Where to buy (Instacart/Walmart links)

### 2. Grocery Order Optimizer (How To Order)

A basket-level optimization workflow:

- Start with current cart.
- Flag calorie-dense low-satiety items.
- Suggest 1:1 replacements preserving craving and convenience.
- Show exact calorie difference per item and per week.
- Export final optimized cart for one-click ordering.

## Constraint Rules

1. No morality language.
2. Preserve taste profile and convenience.
3. Keep friction lower, not higher.
4. Show exact math per replacement.
5. Prioritize repeatability over perfection.

## Required Outputs

- Top grocery swaps by category
- Item-level macro table
- Weekly calorie savings summary
- "Order this now" linkouts
- Time-vs-money objection handler

## Relationship To Other Vectors

- Snack Bible: single-item decisions.
- Grocery Store Bible: household default decisions.
- Fast Food Bible: chain-restaurant decisions.
- Restaurant Bible: non-labeled menus (wild west decisions).
- Trainerize Sync: logging and execution.
