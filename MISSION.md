## The Philosophy

Imagine you didn't change a single thing about what you ate. Imagine you just were aware and stayed curious about every single bite you put in your mouth — every snack, every place to order takeout. You didn't judge it. You didn't try to change it. You didn't go on a diet. You just know it.

Notice what that is, based on what you currently do unconsciously. That is your maintenance calories, by definition, if your weight has stayed the same.

Everything that they're currently doing, without judgment — that is their maintenance calories.

Then the game becomes: each decision, we slightly improve from what you're currently doing while still honoring what you love about that meal, creating that deficit. Every 500 calories we remove from whatever you were currently ordering, assuming it's seven days in the week, is one pound of fat.

## Phase 1: Find Your Maintenance (Weeks 1-2)

Clients in Phase 1 do two things:

1. **Take a photo of every single thing they eat before they eat it.** Your phone eats before you do.
2. **Log comments about WHY they ate it.** Was it convenience? Was it because they love it?

Plus: step on a scale every day to prove their eating is at maintenance.

This brings the unconscious patterns of eating conscious. We identify two things:

- **Leaks** — things they didn't realize were so calorie dense, so small volume, that if they knew, they would make different choices. The full mayo. The olive oil. The bagels.
- **Hidden Gems** — low calorie, high volume, delicious meals they've been eating or would eat, but didn't give themselves permission to because a coach or someone online told them it's not healthy.

The point of Phase 1 is NOT fat loss. It's NOT progress. We're not rushing anywhere. It's a chance to pause and be honest. To collect the data you've always wanted.

### The Snack Bible (Client-Facing Module)

Snacks are not full meals. They are the smallest repeatable decisions that leak calories fast.

**Definition:** A snack is any standalone item eaten outside a full meal context (chips, candy bars, cookies, ice cream servings, convenience-store combos, desk drawer foods).

**Core UX:** Side-by-side comparison only.

- Left: current snack (exact brand, serving size, calories, macros)
- Right: integrity-preserving swap (same craving profile, lower calories, higher protein when possible)
- Savings badges: calories saved per swap, protein gain, and weekly deficit at selected frequency

**Integrity criteria for snack swaps:**

1. Match the craving profile first (spicy crunch, chocolate, creamy dessert, salty, sweet).
2. Keep convenience equal or better (same grab-and-go friction).
3. Prioritize meaningful calorie savings and satiety (protein/fiber) without making the snack unrecognizable.

**Example:**

`Flamin' Hot Cheetos (320 cal) -> Quest Spicy Chili Chips (140 cal, 19g protein)`

This is the snack version of the same philosophy: don't remove what they love, just tighten the math.

### The Dynamic Graph

At the bottom, a graph that dynamically shows how much weight they're going to lose by making all these swaps.

- You can toggle it per item — choose which swaps to include
- Shows calorie deficit per toggled item
- Transitions to total weight loss projection
- Changes the graph in real time

### The Convenience Game

If their comments mention they eat something for convenience — their preferences aren't that specific, they just eat at convenience — then we play the same convenience game.

Let's say they order something from Subway. The easiest, simplest, lowest-friction option, based on atomic habits, is to continue ordering from Subway, because you already have a mental association with Subway. I'm gonna tell you what to order. As long as it's any type of sandwich, you're pretty much good to go.

If someone loves spicy hot Cheetos and I said "swap these for something without the spice" — that's out of integrity. Their comments tell us what matters to them.

### The Integrity Rule

We honor what they love. If they love the spice, we find a spicy swap. If they love the crunch, we find a crunchy swap. If they love a specific restaurant, we optimize within that restaurant. We never take away what makes them happy — we find a smarter version of it.

## Trainerize Sync (The Tracking System)

Once we have alignment on which swaps they want and whatever adjustments they want to make, that same day I create custom meals in their Trainerize fitness app that have the macros — calories, protein, carbs, and fats — of that meal locked in.

We have a meeting where we sign off on those things. Then with one click of a button, they click it in their fitness app and it auto-logs exactly that meal.

### The Simple Rule

I assign, based on their weight loss goal, a weekly fat loss target and a daily calorie number. Their simple job is to eat whatever they want and stop when they hit their number.

Example: I give someone 1,950 calories and they always eat at the Cheesecake Factory. I give them an optimized Cheesecake Factory meal that's pre-logged in their fitness app. They click the plus button and it auto-logs, eating away at the 1,950 calories.

If for any reason they were unable to hit their number that day, they just send me a message and explain the context and why. We create a plan to better learn how to do it for the next day — no judgment. This is so that we continuously refine what is their ideal lifestyle.

## Core Principles

1. **No judgment.** We're not changing anything in Phase 1. We're observing.
2. **Honor what they love.** The swap must respect the original — same flavor profile, same craving satisfaction.
3. **Convenience over willpower.** If they eat at Subway for convenience, we optimize Subway. We don't suggest meal prep.
4. **Data over feelings.** RMR is math. 3,500 cal = 1 lb. The scale is data. No guessing.
5. **Worst-case planning.** We assume sedentary. No workouts. No steps. Just food swaps. Everything above that is bonus.
6. **The deficit is found, not forced.** We find the 500 cal/day gap in what they're already eating — not create an artificial restriction.
7. **Maintaining is easier than earning.** Getting lean takes work. Staying lean is the reward.
8. **Built by them, for them.** Every client's ecosystem is different because every client's life is different.
