(4:35) Your digestive system breaks it down and hands the money to your blood. The blood is the delivery guy — it goes around paying all your organs their "salary." Heart gets 50. Kidneys get 50 each. Brain gets 100. Muscles get 100.

(4:52) After paying everyone off, there's 200 calories left over. And here's the key — your body doesn't throw that away. It deposits it in the fat bank.

(5:30) Yesterday, your body converted calories into fat. Today, it converted fat back into calories.

(5:38) That's it. That's the whole game. If you eat more than your body needs, the extra gets stored. If you eat less, your body pulls from the reserves. Every 3,500 calories your body pulls from the reserves — that's one pound of fat gone.
